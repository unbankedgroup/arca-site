*GoHighLevel hidden fees are the reason the $97 sticker price doesn't match the $150 to $300 most solo consultants actually pay. Here's what they don't tell you upfront.*

---

GoHighLevel markets itself as an all-in-one platform for $97 a month. That number appears on every landing page, every comparison article, every affiliate review.

It's technically accurate. $97 is the base subscription. What it doesn't include is everything that makes the platform actually work.

## The base price: what you get

For $97 per month on the Starter plan, you get the core platform: CRM, pipeline management, website builder, basic automations, calendar, forms, and a few other tools. It's a significant amount of functionality.

But the functionality requires fuel. And the fuel costs extra.

## The charges most people don't see until the first bill

**Email sending: $0.000675 per email.** Sounds tiny until you do the math. If you send 500 emails per week across follow-ups, newsletters, onboarding sequences, and invoices, that's 2,000 emails per month. Cost: $1.35. Not devastating. But scale to a busy practice with 20 clients and automated sequences, and you're sending 8,000 to 15,000 emails per month. Cost: $5.40 to $10.13. Still manageable, but it adds up when combined with everything else.

The bigger issue with email is deliverability. Multiple users on Reddit and in GHL communities report open rates dropping from 35 to 40% down to single digits when using GHL's default email sending. The fix? Add Mailgun or another dedicated email provider. Another $35 to $89 per month.

**SMS sending: $0.0079 per segment.** A single text that exceeds 160 characters becomes two segments. If you use SMS for appointment reminders, follow-ups, and lead responses, costs escalate quickly. Ten SMS messages per client per month across 15 clients: 150 messages at roughly $0.016 each (assuming most are two segments) = $2.40. Add in longer messages and the cost rises.

**Phone calls: $0.013 per minute outbound, $0.0085 per minute inbound.** Each phone number costs $1.15 per month. If you use GHL's phone system for any client communication, you're paying per minute on top of the base.

**Premium actions and add-ons.** Certain workflow actions, AI features, and integrations carry additional per-use charges. The pricing page doesn't list them all. Users discover them when the bill arrives.

## What users actually pay

The consensus across Reddit threads, YouTube breakdowns, and GHL community forums is consistent. Solo users typically pay between $150 and $300 per month. Not $97.

One user shared their breakdown: "$97 base, $42 SMS, $18 email, $12 phone numbers, $35 Mailgun. Total: $204 a month. And my email open rates are still terrible."

Another: "I thought I was saving money by consolidating to GHL. My old stack was $145 a month. GHL costs me $180 and I spend 10 hours a week managing it."

For [solo consultants already spending $115 to $146 on disconnected tools](/blog/saas-subscription-audit/), the promise of consolidation at $97 is compelling. The reality of paying $200 plus spending 30 to 40 hours learning the platform is not.

## The setup cost nobody advertises

The $97 price tag assumes you can set it up yourself. GHL's own community and ecosystem suggest otherwise.

Setup specialists charge $500 to $2,000 to configure GHL for your business. "Snapshots" (pre-built templates) cost $200 to $500. Training courses from GHL gurus run $97 to $997. Community members consistently report that it takes 30 to 40 hours of self-setup to get the basics running, or months if you're learning as you go.

One review put it plainly: "GoHighLevel was built with agencies in mind, so solopreneurs and coaches may feel the tool is too big." The learning curve takes [about a year to reach competency](/blog/gohighlevel-alternative-coaches/) according to experienced users.

For a coach billing $200 per hour, 30 hours of setup time costs $6,000 in opportunity cost. Add the ongoing monthly charges, and the first-year total cost of GHL is closer to $8,400 to $9,600 than the $1,164 the sticker price suggests.

## When GHL makes sense (and when it doesn't)

To be fair: GoHighLevel is powerful. For agencies managing dozens of client sub-accounts, the per-use pricing model makes sense because they pass costs through to clients. The white-label features, funnel builder, and membership tools serve a real market.

For a solo consultant or coach managing their own 10 to 20 clients, most of those features sit unused. You're paying for an agency tool and using maybe 20% of it. The hidden fees add up because you're on your own. No agency infrastructure to absorb the costs. No dedicated ops person to manage the platform.
