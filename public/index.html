FAQ

Questions we hear often.

I've tried all-in-ones before. Why is Arca different? +
Most all-in-ones are built for agencies running dozens of clients, then poorly adapted for solo operators. Arca was built from the ground up for service businesses with 1–15 people. And instead of weeks configuring it, we build it for you in 7 days.
What if I'm not technical? +
Perfect. You're exactly who this is for. If you can send a text, you can use Arca. No dashboards, no routines to configure, no settings. You talk to it in plain English.
Will my clients know I'm using Arca? +
No. Every message, invoice, and reminder that reaches your clients looks like it's coming directly from you. Arca runs the back office. Your personal touch stays front and center.
Can I control what gets sent? +
Yes. Draft review is included in every plan. Arca drafts messages, you approve or edit. Nothing client-facing happens without your say-so, unless you opt into auto-send for low-risk items like appointment confirmations.
What tools does Arca replace? +
Typically: your scheduling tool (Calendly, Acuity), invoicing (FreshBooks, Wave), CRM (HoneyBook, Dubsado), email platform (Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign), and the spreadsheet you were actually using. $150–$400/mo saved before counting time.
Contract or commitment? +
No contracts. Month-to-month. Cancel anytime. 30-day money-back on your first month.
What is the CLOSED Framework? +
CLOSED stands for Capture, Log, Orchestrate, Scan, Execute, Document. It's the operating loop Arca runs on every cycle. Each pass, your operations get sharper because the system learns from what worked and what didn't.
How is Arca different from Zapier or Make? +
Zapier and Make connect apps together. Arca runs your operations end-to-end. Instead of building automations yourself, Arca captures your workflows, orchestrates them, and learns from every cycle. No setup required — we build it for you.
Do I need to set up anything? +
No. We build your Arca for you. After a 30-minute diagnosis call, our team wires up your scheduling, invoicing, onboarding, and follow-ups. You wake up to your first morning brief on day 7.
