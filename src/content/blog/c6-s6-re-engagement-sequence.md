# Re-engagement Sequence for Inactive Clients: Win Back Lost Revenue

Your CRM is full of former clients. People who loved your work. People who got results. People who haven't heard from you in months.

A re-engagement sequence for inactive clients turns that graveyard into revenue. Not by being pushy. By being present.

This guide covers the exact 3-message campaign that reactivates 15-25% of former clients. With templates. With timing. With the psychology that makes it work.

## Why re-engagement works

Former clients are your warmest audience. They know you. They trust you. They've already gotten results.

According to HubSpot research, referred leads convert at 2-3x the rate of cold leads, and the cost per acquisition is 62% lower than paid advertising. Former clients are even warmer than referrals — they've already experienced your work firsthand.

Compare this to cold marketing:

| Source | Conversion Rate | Cost Per Acquisition |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Cold ads | 1-3% | $200-500 |
| Warm referrals | 20-40% | $50-100 |
| Former clients | 15-25% | $0 |

Re-engagement is low-hanging fruit. And most coaches don't pick it.

## When to re-engage

Timing matters. Too soon = desperate. Too late = forgotten.

**Re-engagement windows:**

- **30 days post-completion** — "How's it going?" check-in
- **60 days post-completion** — "Thinking of you" nudge
- **90 days post-completion** — Re-enrollment conversation
- **6 months post-completion** — "New offering" invitation
- **12 months post-completion** — "Annual check-in"

The 30-90 day window is the sweet spot. They're far enough out that the package is complete. Close enough that they remember the value.

## The 3-message re-engagement sequence

Here's the exact sequence that works.

### Message 1: The genuine check-in (Day 30)

**Purpose:** Re-establish connection. No ask. Just care.

**Subject:** How are things going?

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

It's been a month since we wrapped up. Just wanted to check in and see how things are going.

How's the [specific goal they worked on] coming along? Any wins to celebrate?

No need to reply if you're swamped. Just wanted you to know I'm thinking of you and rooting for you.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** No agenda. Genuine interest. Low pressure. They're more likely to respond because you're not selling.

### Message 2: The value add (Day 60)

**Purpose:** Provide value. Stay top of mind. Plant a seed.

**Subject:** Saw this and thought of you

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

I saw [article/resource/podcast] this week and immediately thought of our work on [specific topic].

Here's the link: [URL]

The part about [specific insight] reminded me of what you were working on. Curious if it resonates.

Hope things are going well!

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** You're providing value, not asking for something. You remember their specific situation. You're still in their corner.

### Message 3: The invitation (Day 90)

**Purpose:** Open the door to re-enrollment. No pressure.

**Subject:** Quick question

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

It's been about three months since we worked together. I've been thinking about you and wanted to ask:

Are you still making progress on [goal]? Or has life gotten in the way a bit?

If you're feeling stuck or want to pick things back up, I have space for a few clients this month. No pressure at all — just wanted to extend the invitation.

Either way, I'm cheering for you.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Specific ask. Acknowledges that life happens. No guilt. Clear invitation with an easy out.

## The follow-up (if they respond)

If they respond to any message, engage warmly. Then:

**If they're doing well:**
- Celebrate their wins
- Ask if there's anything else they want to work on
- Mention you have space if they want to continue

**If they're struggling:**
- Offer a single "tune-up" session
- Suggest a shorter re-enrollment package (4-6 sessions)
- Provide one piece of actionable advice

**If they're interested but hesitant:**
- Offer a free 15-min catch-up call
- Mention payment plan options
- Share a relevant success story

The goal isn't to close hard. It's to make re-engagement easy.

## Scaling with automation

You can send these messages manually. Or you can automate them.

**Manual approach:**
- Write each message from scratch
- Personalize based on client history
- Time: 10-15 minutes per message
- Best for: Top 20% of former clients (highest value)

**Automated approach:**
- Templates with merge fields for personalization
- Draft-and-approve before sending
- Time: 2-3 minutes per message (review + approve)
- Best for: All former clients in your CRM

**Hybrid approach (recommended):**
- Automate Messages 1 and 2
- Hand-write Message 3 for high-value clients
- Best of both worlds

## When NOT to re-engage

Some clients shouldn't be re-engaged.

**Don't re-engage if:**
- They left on bad terms
- They explicitly said they don't want to continue
- They didn't pay their final invoice
- They were a poor fit (drained your energy)

Re-engagement is for clients who got value and left on good terms. Not for resurrecting dead relationships.

## FAQ

### How soon after package completion should I re-engage a client?

Start at 30 days with a genuine check-in (no ask). Follow up at 60 days with a value-add message (resource, article). At 90 days, extend a re-enrollment invitation. This 30-90 day window is the sweet spot — they're far enough out that the package is complete, but close enough to remember the value.

### What if they don't respond to the first message?

Continue the sequence. Many clients will read but not respond until Message 2 or 3. The sequence is designed to build warmth over time. Message 1 establishes care. Message 2 provides value. Message 3 extends the invitation. Some won't respond until they're ready.

### How do I personalize re-engagement messages at scale?

Use merge fields for: name, specific goal they worked on, package type, completion date. Reference something specific from their engagement ("our work on boundary-setting," "the leadership transition you were navigating"). The draft-and-approve model lets you review and add personal touches before each message sends.

### What's a good re-engagement conversion rate?

15-25% of former clients re-engaging is realistic. Per 100 clients contacted: 30-40% will read, 10-15% will respond, 5-8% will book a call, 3-5% will re-enroll. For a $3,000/package business, that's $9,000-15,000 from a single sequence.

### Should I automate re-engagement or keep it manual?

Hybrid works best. Automate Messages 1 and 2 for all former clients. Hand-write Message 3 for your top 20% highest-value clients. This gives you scale (all clients get touched) with personalization where it matters most (high-value re-enrollments).
