**Quick answer:** Upsell sequences offer additional services at natural inflection points — when a client achieves a win, nears completion, or expresses a new goal. The best upsells feel like helpful suggestions, not sales pitches. Done right, they increase lifetime value by 40-60%.

---

I used to think upselling was sleazy. Then I realized: not offering a client the next logical step in their journey is worse than offering it.

A client comes to you for coaching. They get results. They're ready for more. If you don't tell them what's next, who will?

Upsell sequences are not about squeezing more money from clients. They're about serving clients who are ready for more.

The difference is timing and framing.

## The upsell timing framework

There are four natural moments to upsell.

### 1. The mid-program milestone (40-60% through)

Client has achieved something tangible. They're engaged. They trust you.

**What to offer:**
- Extended engagement (continue past the original end date)
- Advanced tier (more access, more support)
- Complementary service (add-on that enhances current work)

**Message:**
```
You've made incredible progress on [goal]. You're halfway through and already seeing [result].

Based on where you're headed, I have a suggestion: [offer].

Clients who add [offer] at this stage typically [specific benefit]. It's designed for people who've done the foundation work and are ready to accelerate.

Want to chat about whether it makes sense for you?
```

**Why it works:** They're experiencing momentum. The offer is tied to their progress, not your revenue.

### 2. The completion upsell (last 20% of program)

Client is finishing. They got value. They're thinking "what's next?"

**What to offer:**
- Next-level program (logical progression)
- Maintenance/ongoing support (retain what they built)
- Group coaching/alumni program (lower price point, continued access)

**Message:**
```
We're wrapping up in a few weeks. You've accomplished [specific outcomes].

As you think about what's next, I want to make sure you don't lose momentum. A few options:

1. Continue 1:1 — We keep working together at [frequency] to [goal]
2. Group coaching — Join our alumni cohort for peer support at a lower price point
3. Maintenance mode — Monthly check-ins to ensure you stay on track

Which feels right for where you are?
```

**Why it works:** You're helping them plan the next chapter, not closing a deal.

### 3. The win-based upsell (within 48 hours of a big win)

Client just achieved something major. Promotion. Revenue milestone. Personal breakthrough.

They're riding high. They believe in the work. They're open to more.

**What to offer:**
- Higher-tier package (they've proven they can use what you offer)
- Referral + expansion (they refer someone, get credit toward upgrade)
- Intensive/add-on (deep dive on the thing that just worked)

**Message:**
```
Huge congratulations on [win]! This is exactly what we've been working toward.

I'm curious — now that you've cracked this, what's next?

A lot of clients in your position decide to [offer]. It's designed for people who've mastered [current level] and are ready for [next level].

Worth exploring?
```

**Why it works:** They're in an expansion mindset. The offer matches their energy.

### 4. The stagnation upsell (when progress plateaus)

Client has been with you a while. Progress has slowed. They might churn soon.

**What to offer:**
- Different modality (switch from 1:1 to group, or vice versa)
- Intensive (break through the plateau)
- Referral to someone else (honestly, if you're not the right fit)

**Message:**
```
I've been thinking about your progress. You've done great work on [X], and I notice things have plateaued on [Y].

This is normal. Often it means one of two things:
1. You're ready for a different approach
2. You've extracted the value from this modality

I have a suggestion: [offer]. It's designed for exactly this inflection point.

Alternatively, if you feel you've gotten what you need, I completely respect that. Let's make sure you're set up for continued success either way.
```

**Why it works:** You're naming the elephant in the room. You're offering a path forward, not pressure.

## The upsell message framework

Here's how to structure any upsell offer.

### The "problem-solution-fit" frame:

```
1. Acknowledge where they are
2. Name the next challenge (they may not see it yet)
3. Offer the solution (your service)
4. Make it optional (no pressure)
```

**Example:**
```
You've built a solid foundation with [current work]. (acknowledge)

The next level is usually [new challenge]. (name it)

I have a program designed for exactly that: [offer]. (solution)

No pressure at all — just wanted to put it on your radar. (optional)
```

### The "here's what other clients do" frame:

```
Most clients at your stage decide to [offer]. They find it helps them [benefit].

Totally up to you — but if you're curious, here are the details.
```

This works because social proof without pressure is persuasive.

### The "by the way" frame:

```
One more thing — I mentioned this in passing, but we have [offer] starting next month.

It's for people who've completed [current program] and want to [next goal].

If that's you, let me know. Happy to reserve a spot.
```

Casual. Informational. No hard close.

## What to offer (upsell ladder)

Your upsell should be a logical next step, not a random product.

**Typical upsell ladder:**

| Current Service | Natural Upsell | Price Increase |
|-----------------|----------------|----------------|
| Single session | Package of 6 | 5-10x |
| 6-week program | 12-week intensive | 2x |
| 1:1 coaching | 1:1 + group combo | 1.5x |
| Group coaching | 1:1 upgrade | 3-5x |
| Done-with-you | Done-for-you | 3-10x |
| Core program | Premium/add-ons | 1.3-2x |

**Key principle:** The upsell should feel like a natural progression, not a lateral move.

## Automating upsell sequences

Your system should trigger upsell offers automatically.

**Triggers:**
- Program 50% complete → send mid-program upsell
- Program 80% complete → send completion upsell
- Client reports big win → send win-based upsell within 48 hours
- No progress in 30 days → send stagnation upsell
- Client asks about "what's next" → send relevant offer immediately

**Sequence structure:**
1. Day 0: Initial upsell offer (personalized)
2. Day 3: Follow-up with social proof ("Other clients found this helpful...")
3. Day 7: Final nudge ("Last chance to join this cohort...")
4. If no → mark as "not interested" and stop

Don't be annoying. Three touches max. If they say no (or don't respond), let it go for 90 days.

## FAQ

### What is an upsell sequence?

An upsell sequence is an automated series of offers sent to existing clients at natural inflection points (mid-program, completion, after a win). The goal is to offer additional services that genuinely help the client.

### How often should I upsell?

2-3 times per client engagement, at natural moments (50% complete, 80% complete, after a big win). Don't upsell more than once per quarter — it becomes noise.

### What if a client says no?

Respect it. Mark them as "not interested" for that offer and don't bring it up again for 90 days. They may be ready later.

### Is upselling ethical?

Yes, if you genuinely believe the additional service helps the client. No, if you're pushing something they don't need. The test: would you recommend this to a friend?

### What's the best upsell offer?

The next logical step in the client's journey. If they finished a 6-week program, offer a 12-week intensive. If they're in group coaching, offer 1:1. Match the offer to their progress.
