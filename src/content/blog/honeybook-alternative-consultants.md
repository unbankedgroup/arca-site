## Why consultants are leaving HoneyBook

The price hike was the trigger. But it's not the only reason consultants are switching.

### Reason 1: Rising costs for features you don't use

HoneyBook's 2025 pricing:

| Plan | Old Price | New Price | Increase |
|------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Starter | $19/mo | $36/mo | 89% |
| Essentials | $39/mo | $59/mo | 52% |
| Premium | $59/mo | $79/mo | 34% |

What did you get for the increase? AI features most consultants don't use. Enterprise integrations you don't need. A "premium experience" that feels like a cash grab.

### Reason 2: Limited automation on lower tiers

Starter and Essentials plans have basic email sequences. But real automation — if/then logic, multi-step workflows, conditional triggers — requires Premium or external tools.

For a consultant running discovery call sequences, onboarding flows, and payment reminders, this means either upgrading or stitching together Zapier integrations (which adds cost and complexity).

### Reason 3: Payment processing fees

HoneyBook Payments charges 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction. Stripe charges 2.9% + $0.30. Same rate. But HoneyBook doesn't let you use Stripe directly on lower tiers — you're locked into their processing.

On $10,000/month in consulting revenue, that's $290/month in fees. Not wrong, but not negotiable either.

### Reason 4: Consultant-specific gaps

HoneyBook is built for "creative service businesses" — photographers, designers, event planners. Consultants have different needs:

- Proposal-to-contract workflows (HoneyBook: good)
- Discovery call scheduling and follow-up (HoneyBook: basic)
- Multi-stakeholder approvals (HoneyBook: limited)
- Retainer billing vs project billing (HoneyBook: project-focused)
- Client portal for ongoing work (HoneyBook: basic)

The tool works. But it's not built for consulting workflows.

Strip away the pretty templates. Here's what matters:

2. **Proposal → contract → payment** — Seamless flow, not three separate tools

4. **Retainer and project billing** — Recurring invoices, milestone-based payments, payment plans
5. **Multi-stakeholder management** — Client, their manager, procurement, HR — all informed appropriately

HoneyBook can do some of this. But consultants need all of it, consistently, without paying for features they don't use.
