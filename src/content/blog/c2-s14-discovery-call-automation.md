# Discovery Call Automation: From No-Show to Signed Client

You book a discovery call. The prospect seems excited. They confirm via email.

Then they don't show up.

You wait 5 minutes. 10 minutes. Send a "are you there?" email. Nothing.

You've lost the call. And probably the client.

Discovery call automation fixes this. Automated reminders reduce no-shows by 50-90%. Pre-call prep increases close rates by 20-30%. Post-call follow-up sequences convert 40-60% of "I need to think about it" prospects.

This guide covers the complete discovery call automation sequence. From booking to signed client.

**TL;DR:** Discovery call automation handles: instant booking confirmation, pre-call prep (24hr before), reminders (24hr + 1hr), no-show recovery sequence, and post-call follow-up (Day 1, 3, 5, 7). The result: 50-90% fewer no-shows, 20-30% higher close rates.

## The discovery call automation sequence

### Step 1: Booking confirmation (instant)

**Trigger:** Prospect books a call via Calendly/your scheduler.

**What happens:**
- Calendar invite sent (with Zoom/meeting link)
- Confirmation email sent:
  - Date/time (with timezone)
  - Meeting link
  - What to expect ("We'll discuss X, Y, Z")
  - Pre-call questionnaire link ("Help me prepare for our call")

**Why it matters:** Instant confirmation reduces same-day cancellations. Pre-call questionnaire makes the call more productive (you know their situation upfront).

**Template:**
```
Subject: Confirmed: Your discovery call is [Day] at [Time]

Hi [Name],

Your discovery call is confirmed for [Day, Date] at [Time] [Timezone].

Here's the Zoom link: [link]

Before we talk, can you fill out this quick form? It helps me understand your situation so we can make the most of our time.

[Questionnaire link - 3-5 questions max]

Looking forward to it.

[Your name]
```

### Step 2: Pre-call prep (24 hours before)

**Trigger:** 24 hours before scheduled call.

**What happens:**
- Reminder email sent:
  - "Tomorrow at [Time]"
  - Zoom link (again)
  - Quick prep ("Think about [specific goal/challenge]")
  - Reschedule link (if needed)

**Why it matters:** Reduces no-shows. Gives them time to reschedule instead of ghosting.

**Template:**
```
Subject: Tomorrow: Your discovery call at [Time]

Hi [Name],

Quick reminder that we're talking tomorrow at [Time].

Here's the Zoom link: [link]

Before we meet, think about: What does success look like for you in the next 90 days? That's where we'll start.

If you need to reschedule, no problem: [reschedule link]

See you tomorrow.

[Your name]
```

### Step 3: Final reminder (1 hour before)

**Trigger:** 1 hour before scheduled call.

**What happens:**
- SMS or email reminder:
  - "In 1 hour"
  - Zoom link (one more time)
  - "See you soon!"

**Why it matters:** Last-minute no-shows drop by 50%+ with 1-hour reminders. People forget. Or they get busy. The reminder brings them back.

**Template (email):**
```
Subject: In 1 hour: Discovery call at [Time]

Hi [Name],

See you in an hour!

Zoom link: [link]

[Your name]
```

**Template (SMS):**
```
[Name], see you in 1 hour for your discovery call. Zoom: [short link]
```

### Step 4: No-show recovery (if they don't show)

**Trigger:** Prospect doesn't join within 10 minutes of scheduled time.

**What happens:**
- Immediate email (10 minutes after):
  - "Are you okay?" (not "you missed it")
  - Offer to reschedule
  - No guilt

- Follow-up #1 (24 hours later):
  - "Everything okay?"
  - Reschedule link
  - "No pressure"

- Follow-up #2 (72 hours later):
  - "Last try"
  - Reschedule link
  - "Door's open if you want to reach out"

**Why it matters:** 30-50% of no-shows will reschedule if you make it easy. Most coaches send one awkward email and give up.

**Template (immediate):**
```
Subject: Are you okay? / Our call

Hi [Name],

We had a call scheduled for [Time]. You didn't join, so I wanted to check if everything's okay.

No worries at all — these things happen. If you still want to talk, here's my calendar:

[Reschedule link]

Hope all is well.

[Your name]
```

### Step 5: Post-call follow-up (after the call)

**Trigger:** Call completed.

**What happens:**
- If they said yes (or "I need to think about it"):
  - Day 1 (within 24hr): Thank you + proposal + next steps
  - Day 3: Value add (relevant article, case study, resource)
  - Day 5: Direct question ("Any questions I can answer?")
  - Day 7: Final touch ("No pressure. If timing isn't right, I understand.")

- If they said no:
  - Thank you email
  - Add to nurture sequence (monthly check-in)

**Why it matters:** 80% of sales require 5 follow-ups. 44% of salespeople give up after 1. The follow-up sequence is where deals close.

**Template (Day 1):**
```
Subject: Great talking today / Next steps

Hi [Name],

Really enjoyed our conversation today. I think [specific challenge they mentioned] is totally solvable.

As promised, here's the proposal: [link]

Next steps:
1. Review the proposal
2. Any questions? Reply to this email
3. If you're ready, click "Accept" and we'll get started

Let me know if you have any questions.

[Your name]

P.S. The offer includes [bonus/perk] if you decide by [date].
```

**Template (Day 3):**
```
Subject: Thought of you / [Topic]

Hi [Name],

Saw this and thought of our conversation: [article/case study link]

The part about [specific insight] is exactly what we talked about.

Let me know if you have any questions about the proposal.

[Your name]
```

**Template (Day 5):**
```
Subject: Quick question

Hi [Name],

Any questions I can answer about the proposal?

Happy to hop on a quick call if that helps.

[Your name]
```

**Template (Day 7):**
```
Subject: Timing

Hi [Name],

Just checking in one more time.

If the timing isn't right, I totally understand. No pressure at all.

If you do want to move forward, the proposal is here: [link]

Either way, rooting for you.

[Your name]
```

## Common discovery call automation mistakes

### Mistake 1: Too many reminders

**Wrong:** Email at booking, 48hr, 24hr, 12hr, 1hr, and SMS at 1hr. That's 6 touchpoints. Feels spammy.

**Right:** Email at booking, 24hr, and 1hr. Optional SMS at 1hr. 3-4 touchpoints max.

### Mistake 2: Generic reminders

**Wrong:** "Reminder: You have a call scheduled."

**Right:** "Tomorrow we'll talk about [their specific challenge]. Think about what success looks like in 90 days."

### Mistake 3: No no-show sequence

**Wrong:** One email ("you missed it") and give up.

**Right:** 3-email sequence over 72 hours. Make rescheduling easy. No guilt.

### Mistake 4: Weak post-call follow-up

**Wrong:** Send proposal. Wait. Send "just checking in" emails.

**Right:** Day 1 (proposal), Day 3 (value add), Day 5 (direct question), Day 7 (final touch with easy out).

## FAQ

### How do I reduce discovery call no-shows?

Implement automated reminders: instant confirmation with calendar invite, 24-hour email reminder, 1-hour SMS reminder. This reduces no-shows by 50-90%. Add a pre-call questionnaire to increase commitment.

### What should discovery call follow-up emails say?

Day 1: Thank you + proposal + clear next steps. Day 3: Value add (relevant article/case study). Day 5: Direct question ("Any questions?"). Day 7: Final touch with easy out ("No pressure if timing isn't right").

### How many follow-ups should I send after a discovery call?

Send 4 follow-ups: Day 1, 3, 5, and 7. 80% of sales require 5+ touchpoints. 44% of salespeople give up after 1. The money is in the follow-up.

### Should I use SMS or email for reminders?

Both. Email for detailed reminders (booking confirmation, 24-hour). SMS for last-minute (1-hour). SMS has 98% open rate vs 20% for email. Use SMS sparingly (1-hour reminder only) to avoid feeling intrusive.
