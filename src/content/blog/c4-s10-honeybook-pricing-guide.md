HoneyBook pricing in 2026 ranges from $19 to $80 per month depending on your tier and billing cycle. But the real cost depends on what you need: the Starter plan at $19/month ($192/year) handles basic invoicing and contracts, while Premium at $39/month ($384/year) unlocks automations and unlimited projects.

Then there are the hidden costs: payment processing fees (2.9% + $0.30 per transaction), add-on features, and the time cost of configuration.

Here's the complete breakdown of what HoneyBook costs in 2026, what each tier actually includes, and when a platform like Arca or Dubsado might be the better value.

## HoneyBook's 2026 pricing tiers

HoneyBook simplified its pricing in 2025 after an 89% price increase that upset existing users. The current structure has two main tiers plus a free trial.

### Free Trial
**Cost:** $0 for 7 days

**What you get:**
- Full access to Premium features
- Up to 3 projects
- Sample templates included

**The catch:** You can't actually run a business on it. It's a sandbox. After 7 days, you must upgrade or lose access to your data.

### Starter Plan
**Cost:** $19/month or $192/year (save $36)

**What you get:**
- Unlimited clients and projects
- Basic invoicing and payments
- Contract templates (5 included)
- Inquiry form
- Email support

**What you don't get:**
- Automations (the main reason to use HoneyBook)
- Custom branding
- Advanced reporting
- Priority support

**Who it's for:** Solopreneurs who need contracts and invoices but don't need workflow automation. If you're manually sending everything anyway, Starter works.

### Premium Plan
**Cost:** $39/month or $384/year (save $84)

**What you get:**
- Everything in Starter, plus:
- Unlimited automations
- Custom branding
- Advanced reporting and analytics
- Priority support
- All template library access

**Who it's for:** Coaches and consultants who want automated workflows. This is where HoneyBook becomes functional for a real business.

### Enterprise / Custom
**Cost:** Custom pricing (typically $80+/month)

**What you get:**
- White-label options
- Dedicated account manager
- Custom integrations
- Training and onboarding

**Who it's for:** Agencies or multi-practitioner firms. Most solo coaches never need this.

## The hidden costs nobody mentions

The subscription fee is just the starting point. Here's what HoneyBook actually costs you over a year.

### Payment processing fees

HoneyBook Payments charges 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction. On a $3,000 coaching package, that's $87.30 in fees. If you process $5,000/month in payments ($60,000/year), you're paying:

- Monthly: $148 in processing fees
- Annually: $1,776 in processing fees

This is standard across the industry (Stripe charges the same), but it's worth factoring into your total cost.

### The "upgrade pressure"

Many coaches start on Starter, then realize automations are locked behind Premium. That's a $20/month upgrade you didn't budget for. Over 3 years, that "oops" costs $720.

### Time cost of configuration

HoneyBook requires significant setup time. Users report 20-40 hours to fully configure workflows, templates, and automations. At a coach's billing rate of $150-200/hour, that's:

- 30 hours × $175/hour = $5,250 in opportunity cost

This isn't a fee HoneyBook charges. It's the cost of your time learning and building instead of coaching.

## When HoneyBook is worth it

HoneyBook makes sense for coaches who:

1. **Have 20+ hours for setup** — You're willing to invest the time learning the platform
2. **Need client-facing portals** — HoneyBook's client experience is polished
3. **Want template variety** — The template library is extensive
4. **Value brand customization** — Premium includes white-label options

## When HoneyBook isn't worth it

Consider alternatives if you:

1. **Don't have setup time** — If 30 hours of configuration sounds exhausting, Arca's done-for-you model is better value
2. **Need advanced automations** — HoneyBook's automation builder is basic compared to dedicated tools
3. **Are price-sensitive** — At $39/month plus time cost, HoneyBook isn't the budget option it appears to be
4. **Want flat pricing** — HoneyBook has raised prices multiple times. [Flat pricing platforms](/blog/c4-s6-flat-pricing-no-credits/) offer predictability.

## The 89% price increase controversy

In 2024, HoneyBook announced an 89% price increase for existing users. Some coaches went from $20/month to $38/month overnight. The backlash was significant:

- Facebook group posts with hundreds of angry comments
- Migration threads ("What are you switching to?")
- Trust erosion among long-time users

HoneyBook walked back some increases after the backlash, but the incident revealed a risk: your operations platform can change pricing anytime. If you've built your business on their workflows, switching is painful.

This is why some coaches prefer platforms with transparent, stable pricing or done-for-you models where the service is tied to outcomes, not features.

## The real question: tool or service?

HoneyBook is a tool you operate. You get the features. You build the workflows. You maintain the automations. When something breaks, you fix it.

The alternative is a service model: you describe your business, someone builds the operations for you, and you approve the results. That's what Arca offers at $97/month.

**Tool model (HoneyBook/Dubsado):**
- Lower monthly cost ($39-50/month)
- Higher time cost (30+ hours setup + ongoing maintenance)
- You're the system administrator

**Service model (Arca):**
- Higher monthly cost ($97/month)
- Lower time cost (7-day setup, done for you)
- Someone else is the system administrator

For coaches billing $150-300/hour, the service model often has better total value. The $58/month premium recovers 10+ hours of admin time in the first month alone.

## Bottom line: what should you pay?

**Choose HoneyBook Starter ($19/month) if:** You need contracts and invoices, don't need automations, and have time to learn the platform.

**Choose HoneyBook Premium ($39/month) if:** You want automated workflows, have 20-40 hours for setup, and value client-facing polish.

**Consider Arca ($97/month) if:** You'd rather pay for done-for-you setup than spend 30 hours configuring workflows yourself.

**Consider Dubsado ($50/month) if:** You want more automation flexibility and don't mind a steeper learning curve.

The cheapest option isn't always the lowest total cost. Factor in your time. Then decide what kind of operator you want to be: builder or delegator.

## FAQ

### How much does HoneyBook cost in 2026?

HoneyBook Starter is $19/month or $192/year. HoneyBook Premium is $39/month or $384/year. Payment processing (2.9% + $0.30) is additional. Custom/Enterprise pricing is available for teams.

### Is HoneyBook worth the price increase?

Depends on your needs. If you use automations heavily and value the client portal, Premium at $39/month is reasonable. If you're mostly using it for invoices and contracts, Starter or a cheaper alternative may suffice.

### What's the difference between HoneyBook Starter and Premium?
