If you're comparing HoneyBook vs Dubsado vs Arca for coaches, you're probably at the stage where sticky notes and spreadsheets stopped working. You need a system. But these three platforms solve the problem differently, and the right one depends on what you actually need.

I'll be honest about where each platform wins and where it falls short. I built Arca, so I have a bias. I'll be transparent about it.

## The short version

HoneyBook is the fastest to get running. Dubsado gives you the most control over customization. Arca manages your operations for you so you don't have to run the system yourself.

Each one trades something for something else. Speed for flexibility. Control for simplicity. Cost for time. Here's how it breaks down.

## HoneyBook: fast, polished, but limited

HoneyBook is built for creative entrepreneurs and service providers who want a clean, modern platform they can set up in a weekend. It does that well.

The interface is intuitive. Invoicing and payment processing work out of the box. Scheduling is built in. For coaches who want a professional-looking client experience with minimal friction, HoneyBook delivers.

Where it falls short is automation depth. HoneyBook's automation is template-based. You can trigger emails when a form is submitted or a payment is received. But conditional logic, multi-step sequences, and branching flows are limited. If your follow-up process has any complexity, you'll hit the ceiling fast.

The pricing has also climbed significantly. Starter costs $36 per month. Essentials costs $59 per month. Premium costs $109 per month. For coaches who started on the platform when it was cheaper, the [price increases have been frustrating](/blog/c4-s2-honeybook-alternative/). And at the Premium tier, you're paying over $1,300 a year for a tool you still have to operate yourself.

HoneyBook wins on: fast setup, clean design, built-in payments, and a smooth client experience.

HoneyBook struggles with: complex automation, rising costs, and limited customization of client-facing documents.

## Dubsado: deeply customizable, but time-intensive

Dubsado is the opposite trade-off. Where HoneyBook prioritizes speed, Dubsado prioritizes control. You can customize nearly everything. Forms, proposals, contracts, workflows, client portals, email sequences. If you can imagine it, Dubsado probably lets you build it.

The problem is the "build it" part. Setting up Dubsado properly takes weeks, not hours. The workflow builder is powerful but complex. Most coaches I've talked to either hire a Dubsado setup specialist (typically $500 to $2,000) or abandon their setup halfway through.

For coaches who complete the setup, Dubsado runs reliably. The [customization depth](/blog/c4-s3-dubsado-alternative/) is genuinely impressive. But the time investment is real, and you need to maintain it. When your process changes, you rebuild the workflow.

Pricing is more accessible. Starter costs $20 per month. Premier costs $40 per month. That's significantly less than HoneyBook or Arca. If budget is the primary constraint and you have the time to invest in setup, Dubsado offers strong value.

Dubsado wins on: customization, form design, contract management, and price.

Dubsado struggles with: setup time, learning curve, mobile experience, and modern automation capabilities.

## Arca: managed operations, not another tool

I built Arca because I kept meeting coaches and consultants who had tried both HoneyBook and Dubsado and still felt like they were spending half their week on admin. The tools worked. But the owners were still the ones operating them.

Arca works on a different model. Instead of giving you a platform and expecting you to configure it, we build your operations for you during a 7-Day Build. You describe how your business runs. We set up the Routines, the Connections, the follow-up sequences, the onboarding flows, the payment reminders. All of it.

The interface is chat-first. When something needs to change, you describe it. When you want to know your numbers, you ask. You don't log into a dashboard and click through menus.

Pricing is higher than both HoneyBook and Dubsado. Foundation costs $97 per month. Structure costs $247 per month. Fortress costs $497 per month. That's flat. No per-email charges. No per-SMS charges. No credits that run out mid-month. But it's more money up front than either competitor, and I won't pretend otherwise.

The question is what you're paying for. HoneyBook and Dubsado charge for access to a tool. Arca charges for managed operations. You're paying for the system and the work of keeping it running.

Arca wins on: setup speed, ongoing management, flat pricing, and removing the need to learn a platform.

Arca struggles with: higher entry cost, less direct control for people who want to build their own workflows, and being newer to the market.

## Which one fits your coaching business?

This depends on three things: your budget, your time, and how much you want to be involved in running the system.

**Choose HoneyBook if** you want something polished and working by this weekend. You're comfortable with basic automation. Your coaching business is relatively straightforward with standard scheduling, invoicing, and follow-up needs. And you don't mind paying more as the platform adds features and raises prices.

**Choose Dubsado if** you want maximum control over every client-facing document and workflow. You have the time (or budget to hire a specialist) for a thorough setup. Your business has specific processes that need custom forms and conditional logic. And you prioritize keeping monthly costs low.

**Choose Arca if** you don't want to learn, build, or maintain a platform. You want your operations set up and running within a week. You prefer describing what you need in plain language over clicking through menus. And you'd rather pay more for managed operations than pay less for a tool you have to run yourself.

## The honest differentiator

HoneyBook and Dubsado are tools you operate. You log in. You build. You maintain. You troubleshoot. They're good tools, and for the right person, either one works.

Arca operates your business. You talk to it. It handles the rest.

That's not better or worse in absolute terms. It's a different answer to a different question. HoneyBook and Dubsado answer "what tool should I use to run my business?" Arca answers "who runs my business operations so I can coach?"

If you're still deciding, the [coaching business automation guide](/blog/c2-pillar-coaching-automation-guide/) walks through what operations actually look like when they're working. And the [best operations platforms for 2026](/blog/c4-pillar-best-operations-platforms/) covers the broader landscape beyond these three.

The right platform is the one that gives you your time back. How it does that matters less than whether it actually does.

## FAQ

### Which platform is best for solo coaches?

HoneyBook for fast setup (1-2 days, $36-109/mo). Dubsado for maximum customization ($20-40/mo, 2-6 week setup). Arca for done-for-you operations ($97-497/mo, 7-day setup). Match the platform to your builder tolerance and budget.

### How much do coaching platforms cost?

HoneyBook: $36-109/mo. Dubsado: $20-40/mo. Arca: $97-497/mo flat. GoHighLevel: $97 base, $150-300 real cost with email/SMS charges. Factor in setup time: HoneyBook (1-2 days), Dubsado (2-6 weeks or $500-2,000 specialist), Arca (7 days done-for-you).

### What's the difference between HoneyBook and Dubsado?

HoneyBook prioritizes speed and simplicity (1-2 day setup, polished interface). Dubsado prioritizes customization and control (2-6 week setup, deep workflow builder). HoneyBook for coaches who want it running fast. Dubsado for coaches who enjoy building systems.

### Can I migrate from HoneyBook or Dubsado to Arca?

Yes. Export client data, contracts, and templates as CSVs. Arca's 7-Day Build reconstructs your workflows as Routines. Your templates become Blueprints. Migration is done for you, not DIY.

### Do I need a setup specialist for Dubsado?

Most users hire one ($500-2,000) because the workflow builder is complex. HoneyBook requires no specialist. Arca includes setup in the monthly price. Factor specialist cost into total cost of ownership.
