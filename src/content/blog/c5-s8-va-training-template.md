# Virtual Assistant Training Template: The SOP Framework That Actually Works

You hired a VA. Now you need to train them. But you don't have a system. You're winging it. And winging it costs 40+ hours of your time.

This virtual assistant training template gives you the framework. Not theory. The actual SOP structure, onboarding checklist, and training timeline that works for service businesses.

Use this as-is. Or adapt it to your business. Either way, you'll save 20-30 hours on training and reduce the "they don't quite get it" frustration.

## The training timeline

A proper VA onboarding takes 4-6 weeks. Not full-time. But structured.

| Week | Focus | Time Investment |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Week 1 | Orientation + tool access | 8-10 hours |
| Week 2 | Core processes (shadow mode) | 8-10 hours |
| Week 3 | Core processes (guided practice) | 5-8 hours |
| Week 4 | Independent work + review | 3-5 hours |
| Week 5-6 | Optimization + advanced tasks | 2-4 hours |

**Total:** 26-37 hours over 4-6 weeks.

At $200/hour, that's $5,200-7,400 in training time. This is why documentation matters.

## The SOP template structure

Every Standard Operating Procedure should follow this structure. Not because it's pretty. Because it answers every question a new VA will have.

### SOP Template

```
# [Process Name]

## Purpose
[Why does this process exist? What outcome does it drive?]

## Frequency
[How often does this happen? Daily? Weekly? Per-event?]

## Time Sensitivity
[How quickly must this be done? Within 1 hour? 24 hours? End of day?]

## Tools Required
[List every tool the VA needs access to]

## Trigger
[What starts this process? A form submission? A calendar event? A specific date?]

## Steps

### Step 1: [Action]
- [Specific instruction]
- [Screenshot or Loom link if helpful]
- [What to look for / decision criteria]

### Step 2: [Action]
- [Specific instruction]
- [Screenshot or Loom link if helpful]
- [What to look for / decision criteria]

[Continue for all steps...]

## Decision Points
[Where does the VA need to make a judgment? What are the criteria?]

Example:
- If lead mentions budget < $1,000, send Tier 1 package
- If lead mentions budget > $5,000, flag for founder review

## Escalation Rules
[When should the VA handle it themselves vs. ask for help?]

Example:
- Handle: Standard inquiries, scheduling, invoice questions
- Escalate: Refund requests, angry clients, technical issues

## Success Criteria
[What does "done" look like? How does the VA know they did it right?]

Example:
- Lead receives response within 2 hours
- Calendar invite includes Zoom link and prep instructions
- Invoice is sent with correct amount and payment terms

## Common Issues + Solutions
[What typically goes wrong? How do they fix it?]

Example:
- Issue: Calendly shows wrong timezone
- Solution: Check Settings > Availability > Timezone in Calendly

## Related SOPs
[Links to other SOPs that connect to this one]

Example:
- [Lead Response SOP]
- [Discovery Call Prep SOP]
- [Client Onboarding SOP]
```

## The onboarding checklist

Use this checklist to track your VA's progress through training.

### Week 1: Orientation

- [ ] Welcome call (30-60 min)
  - [ ] Business overview: what we do, who we serve
  - [ ] Communication preferences and expectations
  - [ ] Review of this training plan
- [ ] Tool access setup
  - [ ] Email (Google Workspace or Microsoft 365)
  - [ ] CRM (HubSpot, Dubsado, etc.)
  - [ ] Scheduling (Calendly, etc.)
  - [ ] Communication (Slack, WhatsApp, etc.)
  - [ ] Password manager (1Password, LastPass)
  - [ ] Project management (Asana, ClickUp, etc.)
- [ ] SOP library walkthrough
  - [ ] Where to find SOPs
  - [ ] How to use the SOP template
  - [ ] How to request updates or clarifications
- [ ] Shadow founder on 2-3 real tasks
  - [ ] Take notes
  - [ ] Ask questions in real-time

### Week 2: Core Processes (Shadow Mode)

VA watches founder do the work, then documents it.

- [ ] Lead response process
  - [ ] Watch founder respond to 3-5 leads
  - [ ] Draft response templates
  - [ ] Get approval on templates
- [ ] Scheduling process
  - [ ] Watch founder handle 3-5 scheduling requests
  - [ ] Practice with test bookings
  - [ ] Learn cancellation/rescheduling rules
- [ ] Invoice creation
  - [ ] Watch founder create 3-5 invoices
  - [ ] Learn pricing/packages
  - [ ] Practice with test invoices
- [ ] Client onboarding
  - [ ] Watch founder onboard 1-2 clients
  - [ ] Learn welcome sequence
  - [ ] Practice sending intake forms

### Week 3: Core Processes (Guided Practice)

VA does the work, founder reviews before sending.

- [ ] Lead response (founder reviews before sending)
- [ ] Scheduling (founder confirms before confirming)
- [ ] Invoice creation (founder approves before sending)
- [ ] Client onboarding (founder reviews all communications)
- [ ] Daily check-in call (15 min, end of day)
  - [ ] Review what got done
  - [ ] Answer questions
  - [ ] Set priorities for tomorrow

### Week 4: Independent Work + Review

VA does the work, founder spot-checks.

- [ ] Lead response (independent, spot-check 50%)
- [ ] Scheduling (independent, spot-check 50%)
- [ ] Invoice creation (independent, spot-check 50%)
- [ ] Client onboarding (independent, spot-check 50%)
- [ ] Weekly review call (30 min)
  - [ ] Review quality
  - [ ] Address patterns of errors
  - [ ] Celebrate wins

### Week 5-6: Optimization + Advanced Tasks

- [ ] Advanced processes
  - [ ] Follow-up sequences
  - [ ] Re-engagement campaigns
  - [ ] Client check-ins
  - [ ] Report generation
- [ ] Exception handling
  - [ ] Angry client responses
  - [ ] Refund requests
  - [ ] Technical issues
- [ ] Process improvement
  - [ ] VA suggests optimizations
  - [ ] Update SOPs based on learnings

## The communication template

Set clear expectations for how and when to communicate.

### Daily Check-In Format (Slack or email)

```
**End of Day Report — [Date]**

**Completed Today:**
- [Task 1]
- [Task 2]
- [Task 3]

**In Progress:**
- [Task] — [Status] — [Expected completion]

**Blockers/Questions:**
- [Question 1]
- [Question 2]

**Priorities for Tomorrow:**
- [Priority 1]
- [Priority 2]
```

### Question Batching

Instead of asking questions throughout the day, batch them.

**Batch windows:** 11am and 4pm [your timezone]

VA sends all non-urgent questions during these windows. Founder responds within 1-2 hours.

**Urgent questions** (client emergencies, time-sensitive issues): Message immediately.

### Weekly Review Agenda

```
**Weekly Review — [Week of Date]**

**Review:**
- Quality check results (founder provides feedback)
- Metrics: response times, completion rates, errors

**Discuss:**
- What's working well
- What's frustrating
- What needs clearer documentation

**Plan:**
- Priorities for next week
- Any new processes to learn
- Any SOPs to update
```

## The VA training video library

Record these once. Use them for every new VA.

**Essential videos:**
1. Business overview (10-15 min)
   - What we do
   - Who we serve
   - Our values and tone

2. Tool walkthroughs (5-10 min each)
   - CRM basics
   - Scheduling tool
   - Invoicing tool
   - Communication tools

3. Core process demos (10-15 min each)
   - Lead response
   - Scheduling
   - Invoice creation
   - Client onboarding

4. Tone and voice (10 min)
   - How we communicate with clients
   - Examples of good vs. bad responses
   - Common mistakes to avoid

Store these in a shared drive (Google Drive, Dropbox, Notion). Organize by category. Link them in the relevant SOPs.

## When to automate instead of train

Here's the uncomfortable truth. Some of what you're training your VA to do should be automated.

**Train the VA on:**
- Judgment calls
- Complex client communications
- Creative work
- Exception handling

**Automate instead of train:**
- Lead response timing (platform responds in 5 minutes)
- Invoice generation (platform creates and sends automatically)
- Payment reminders (platform sends on schedule)
- Follow-up sequences (platform triggers based on events)
- Client onboarding flows (platform runs the sequence)

The [hybrid model](/blog/va-vs-platform/va-vs-operations-platform-guide) trains your VA on high-value work. The platform handles the repeatable execution.

## The bottom line

VA training takes 26-37 hours over 4-6 weeks. Use this template to make it count.

Document everything. Train systematically. Review consistently. And automate the tasks that shouldn't require training in the first place.

## FAQ

### How long does VA training take?

26-37 hours over 4-6 weeks. Week 1: orientation (8-10 hours). Week 2: shadow mode (8-10 hours). Week 3: guided practice (5-8 hours). Week 4: independent work (3-5 hours). Weeks 5-6: optimization (2-4 hours).

### What should VA SOPs include?

Purpose, frequency, time sensitivity, tools required, trigger, step-by-step instructions, decision points, escalation rules, success criteria, common issues + solutions, related SOPs.

### What's the VA onboarding checklist?

Week 1: welcome call, tool access, SOP library, shadow founder. Week 2: core processes in shadow mode. Week 3: guided practice with review. Week 4: independent work with spot-checks. Weeks 5-6: advanced tasks and optimization.

### How can I reduce VA training time?

Use video library (record once, reuse). Batch questions into 2 daily windows. Start with documented SOPs. Use platform for automatable tasks so VA focuses on judgment work from day one.

### What should I automate instead of training VA on?

Lead response timing, invoice generation, payment reminders, follow-up sequences, client onboarding flows. These run better on a platform and don't require 26-37 hours of training.
