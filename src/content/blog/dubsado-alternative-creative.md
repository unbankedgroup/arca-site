## Why creatives are leaving Dubsado

Dubsado is powerful. But power comes with complexity. Here's why creatives are switching:

### Reason 1: Setup takes 2-4 weeks (minimum)

Dubsado isn't a tool you "set up." It's a tool you configure, customize, and engineer.

- Week 1: Watch tutorials, understand workflow logic
- Week 2: Build forms, proposals, contracts
- Week 3: Create automation workflows (if/then triggers)
- Week 4: Test, break, fix, test again

That's 20-40 hours. For a designer billing $100-150/hour, that's $2,000-6,000 in opportunity cost.

### Reason 2: "Dubsado setup specialist" shouldn't exist

When a tool is complex enough that people build careers configuring it for others, that's a red flag.

You don't need a "HoneyBook setup specialist." Or a "Calendly setup specialist." You need a tool that works out of the box.

### Reason 3: Over-engineered for most creatives

Dubsado's strength is deep workflow automation. But most creatives need:
- Beautiful proposals
- Easy contracts
- Simple invoicing
- Basic follow-ups

You're not running a 12-step client journey with conditional branching. You're sending a proposal and collecting payment.

### Reason 4: Dated interface

The Dubsado interface looks like 2015. For creatives who care about aesthetics — who sell visual excellence — this creates cognitive dissonance.

Your client receives a gorgeous proposal... with a clunky, dated signing experience. The tool doesn't reflect your brand.

## The real question: Do you need Dubsado at all?

Dubsado is for service businesses who:
- Need complex workflow automation (if/then/else logic)
- Have multi-step client journeys (8+ touchpoints)
- Want deep customization (CSS, custom fields, conditional logic)
- Have time or budget for setup (DIY or hire specialist)

Creative service businesses are:
- Solo practitioners or small studios
- Selling projects, not ongoing services
- Needing beautiful proposals more than complex workflows
- Valuing speed over customization

If you're a creative, Dubsado works. But it's overkill. Alternatives like Arca and HoneyBook are faster, simpler, and built for your needs.
