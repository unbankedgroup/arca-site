## What happened to HoneyBook pricing

If you are searching for a HoneyBook alternative after price increase, here is the context. In 2025, HoneyBook raised its Starter plan from $19 to $36 per month. That is an 89% increase. The Essentials plan went from $39 to $59. Users who signed up two years ago are now paying 4 to 8 times what they originally budgeted for. The frustration is not about the dollar amount alone. It is about paying significantly more for features that were not requested.

## Why coaches loved HoneyBook in the first place

I want to be fair here. HoneyBook built something genuinely useful for service providers. The proposal-to-contract-to-invoice flow is smooth. A client receives one document, reviews the scope, signs the contract, and pays the deposit. All in one step. That experience is hard to replicate with cobbled-together tools.

Payment collection works well. Automated payment reminders save real time. The client portal gives your buyers a clean, professional experience. For coaches running 1-on-1 or group programs, that unified flow removed a lot of friction.

None of that disappears because the pricing changed. But pricing changes the math on whether you can justify staying.

## The math that changed

A coach paying $19 per month for HoneyBook Starter was getting solid value. At $36 per month, the same feature set costs $432 per year. Essentials at $59 per month runs $708 per year. The Premium plan sits at $109 per month, or $1,308 annually.

For context, many coaches are running lean. A $400 to $700 annual increase hits differently when your monthly revenue fluctuates between $3,000 and $8,000. The price hike did not come with proportional new features. It came with the same tool at a higher number.

The [real cost of your tool stack](/blog/calendly-freshbooks-honeybook-cost/) is worth calculating. If HoneyBook is your primary platform and the price jumped, you need to know what you are actually getting per dollar. Not what you were getting. What you are getting now.

## What HoneyBook does not do

Here is where the comparison matters. HoneyBook handles client workflow management well. Proposals, contracts, invoices, scheduling. But it stops at the edges of your operation.

Follow-up sequences after a lead goes cold? Limited. Automated re-engagement when a past client has not booked in 90 days? Not built for that. Morning briefings that tell you which proposals are pending, which invoices are overdue, and which leads need attention today? That is outside HoneyBook's scope.

HoneyBook manages the transaction. It does not manage the operation around the transaction. For coaches, the operation is where revenue leaks happen. The lead who filled out your inquiry form three weeks ago and never heard back. The past client who would rebook if someone reminded them your calendar is open.

Those gaps cost more than any monthly subscription.

## Who should stay on HoneyBook

If you are a creative professional, primarily doing project-based work with clear start and end dates, and the proposal-to-payment flow is the core of your operations, HoneyBook might still make sense at the new price. Wedding photographers, event planners, and designers with predictable project scopes get genuine value from that unified document flow.

The coaches leaving tend to be the ones whose business is relationship-based, not project-based. Ongoing clients. Recurring revenue. Operations that need to run between engagements. For that model, HoneyBook was always slightly off-center. The price hike just made the misalignment impossible to ignore.

## FAQ

### Why did HoneyBook raise prices so much?

In 2025, HoneyBook increased Starter from $19 to $36/month (89% hike) and Essentials from $39 to $59/month. Existing users reported paying 4-8x their original price. The increases funded new features many users don't need, pushing coaches toward simpler or more predictable alternatives.

### What's the cheapest alternative to HoneyBook?

Dubsado at $20/month is the lowest sticker price. But factor in setup: Dubsado often requires a $500-2,000 specialist. Arca at $97/month includes done-for-you setup (saving specialist fees) and ongoing management. Cheapest upfront vs cheapest total cost are different answers.

### Can I migrate from HoneyBook without losing client data?

Yes. Export client lists, contracts, invoices, and templates as CSVs before canceling. The workflows need rebuilding on any new platform. Done-for-you services (Arca) handle migration. DIY requires rebuilding each workflow manually on the new platform.

### Is HoneyBook still worth it after the price increase?

For creative professionals who need polished proposals and payment processing: yes, Starter at $36/month still delivers value. For coaches needing automation: Premium at $109/month competes with platforms offering more. The value equation changed — evaluate against total cost, not just features.
