## The question every growing agency faces

You're at 10-15 clients. Onboarding is chaotic. Invoices go out late. Client check-ins are slipping. You're working nights to keep up.

You need help. The question is: hire a virtual assistant or use an operations platform?

VA vs operations platform for agencies has a different answer than for coaches or consultants. Agencies have multiple clients to manage simultaneously. More stakeholders. More moving parts. More complexity.

## What agencies use VAs for

Most agencies hire VAs for these tasks:

**Client onboarding:** Sending welcome packets. Collecting assets and access. Scheduling kickoff calls. Setting up project management boards.

**Client communication:** Responding to client inquiries. Scheduling check-in calls. Sending status updates.

**Project coordination:** Tracking deliverables. Chasing client assets. Updating project boards. Coordinating between team members.

**Invoice creation:** Generating invoices after milestones. Sending payment reminders. Chasing overdue accounts.

**Reporting:** Compiling campaign data. Creating client reports. Formatting presentations.

**Lead management:** Responding to inquiries. Scheduling discovery calls. Sending proposals. Following up on sent proposals.

At 10-15 hours/week, a VA costs $600-1,500/month for offshore help. At 40 hours/week, a full-time VA costs $2,400-4,000/month all-in.

## What agencies use platforms for

Operations platforms handle the same tasks differently:

**Client onboarding:** Platform sends welcome sequence automatically. Collects assets via intake form. Schedules kickoff call. Creates project board. All triggered when contract is signed.

**Client communication:** Platform sends scheduled check-ins. Reminds clients of upcoming deadlines. Requests assets at the right time. VA handles complex conversations.

**Invoicing:** Platform generates and sends invoices on milestone trigger. Sends payment reminders at 7/14/30 days. Flags overdue accounts for VA follow-up.

**Reporting:** Platform compiles data from all tools. Delivers white-label report to client chat. VA adds commentary and recommendations.

**Lead management:** Platform responds within 5 minutes. Qualifies leads. Books discovery call. Sends proposal. Follows up at 3/7/14 days. VA handles negotiation and closing.

At $97-497/month, a platform costs less than a part-time VA. And it runs 168 hours/week vs a VA's 40 hours.

## The cost comparison for agencies

Let's compare real costs at different client volumes.

### At 5-10 clients (roughly $50-150K/year)

| Model | Monthly Cost | Coverage |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Part-time VA (15 hrs/week) | $900-1,500 | 40 hrs/week |
| Operations platform | $97-247 | 168 hrs/week |
| Hybrid (platform + 5 VA hrs) | $400-700 | 168 hrs + human judgment |

**Winner at this stage:** Platform or light hybrid. You don't have enough volume for a full VA.

### At 10-20 clients (roughly $150-400K/year)

| Model | Monthly Cost | Coverage |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Full-time VA | $2,400-4,000 | 40 hrs/week |
| Operations platform | $247-497 | 168 hrs/week |
| Hybrid (platform + 15 VA hrs) | $1,200-1,800 | 168 hrs + human judgment |

**Winner at this stage:** Hybrid. Platform handles operations. VA handles client coordination and exceptions.

### At 20+ clients (roughly $400K+/year)

| Model | Monthly Cost | Coverage |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| 2-3 full-time VAs | $4,800-12,000 | 80-120 hrs/week |
| Operations platform | $497 | 168 hrs/week |
| Hybrid (platform + 25-40 VA hrs) | $2,000-4,000 | 168 hrs + human judgment |

**Winner at this stage:** Hybrid with heavy platform use. Scale without linear cost increases.

## The task breakdown for agencies

Not all agency tasks are created equal. Some need a human. Some need a machine.

### Automate these (platform wins):

- Lead response within 5 minutes
- Client onboarding sequences
- Milestone-based invoicing
- Payment reminders and late notices
- Scheduled client check-ins
- Asset collection reminders
- Report delivery
- Contract renewal notices

### Delegate these (VA wins):

- Complex client communications (scope changes, difficult conversations)
- Custom proposal creation for enterprise deals
- Cross-team coordination
- Exception handling when projects go off-track
- Strategic client consultations
- Escalation management

### Hybrid approach (both):

- Qualified lead outreach (platform qualifies, VA personalizes)
- Client reporting (platform compiles data, VA adds insights)
- Onboarding (platform runs sequence, VA handles exceptions)
- Invoice follow-up (platform sends reminders, VA handles negotiations)

## The 60-70% rule for agencies

Here's the number that matters.

60-70% of a typical agency VA's time goes to tasks that could be automated.

That means if you're paying a VA $3,000/month, you're spending $1,800-2,100/month on work a $97-497/month platform handles better.

This isn't the VA's fault. They didn't choose to spend their time on invoice reminders and asset chasing. They signed up to do meaningful client work. But when operations depend on a person, the person ends up doing everything — including the stuff that should run automatically.

## The hybrid model for agencies

Here's what works best for most agencies at $150-400K/year:

**Platform handles:**
- Lead response within 5 minutes (any time of day)
- Client onboarding sequences
- Milestone invoicing and payment chasing
- Scheduled client check-ins
- Report delivery
- Contract renewal notices
- Daily briefings

**VA handles (10-15 hours/week):**
- Complex client communications
- Custom proposal creation
- Cross-team coordination
- Exception handling
- Strategic client consultations

**Monthly cost:** $1,200-1,800 total.
**Coverage:** 168 hours/week for ops + human judgment for relationships.

## When agencies should hire a VA first

Platform-first is usually the answer. But there are exceptions.

**Hire a VA first if:**
- You have 20+ hours/week of genuinely high-value coordination work
- You're at $300K+ revenue and can afford $3,000+/month
- Your primary bottleneck is cross-team coordination, not operations
- You have complex enterprise clients requiring white-glove handling

**Use a platform first if:**
- You're under $200K/year revenue
- Your primary bottleneck is time spent on admin (onboarding, invoicing, follow-ups)
- You don't want to manage employees
- You want operations running in 7 days, not 4-7 weeks

## The transition playbook

If you currently have a VA and want to introduce automation:

**Week 1-2: Audit together.** List every task your VA handles. Categorize as automatable or human-required.

**Week 3-4: Pilot automation.** Set up the platform for 2-3 automatable tasks. Run parallel with VA's current workflow.

**Week 5-6: Transition.** Move automatable tasks to the platform. VA shifts to higher-value work.

**Week 7+: Re-deploy.** VA focuses on client relationships, coordination, and strategic support.

Most VAs are relieved by this transition. They didn't sign up to chase assets and send invoice reminders. They signed up to do work that matters.

## FAQ

### Should an agency hire a VA or use automation?

Start with automation for operations (onboarding, invoicing, check-ins, reporting). Add a part-time VA (10-15 hrs/week) for complex communications and coordination. Hybrid model costs 50-60% less than full-time VA.

### How much does a VA cost for an agency?

Part-time VA (15 hrs/week): $900-1,500/month offshore. Full-time VA: $2,400-4,000/month all-in. Factor in 2-3x multiplier for training, management, tool seats, turnover.

### What agency tasks should be automated?

Client onboarding sequences, milestone invoicing, payment reminders, scheduled check-ins, asset collection reminders, report delivery, contract renewal notices, lead response within 5 minutes.

### Can a platform replace my agency VA?

For 60-70% of typical VA work, yes. Platforms handle repeatable operations better (faster, 24/7, no mistakes). Keep VA for judgment work: complex communications, coordination, exceptions, strategic consultations.

### What's the best operations platform for agencies?

Depends on your needs. Arca ($97-497/mo) for done-for-you setup and managed operations. HoneyBook ($36-109/mo) for DIY client management. Hybrid model (platform + part-time VA) works best for most agencies at $150-400K/year.
