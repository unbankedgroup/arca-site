The sticker price for a virtual assistant sounds irresistible. $5 per hour for a Filipino VA. $15 per hour for someone in Eastern Europe. $25-50 per hour for US-based help.

At 20 hours per week, that's $400-2,000 per month. Affordable. Manageable. A no-brainer for getting 20 hours of your week back.

Here's the number nobody talks about: the real cost is 2-3 times the sticker price. That $400 per month VA? Actually costs $800-1,200. That $2,000 per month VA? Actually costs $4,000-6,000 when you count everything.

This virtual assistant cost breakdown shows you where the hidden costs live. Not to scare you away from hiring help. To make sure you're budgeting for reality, not a fantasy number.

## The sticker price vs. the real price

Let's start with what you see on the job posting, then walk through what you actually pay.

### Offshore VA (Philippines, India, Pakistan)

**Sticker price:** $5-15 per hour
**20 hours/week:** $400-1,200 per month

**Hidden costs:**
- Training time: 20-40 hours of your time at your hourly rate
- Management: 3-5 hours per week ongoing
- Tool seats: $80-600 per month across 4-6 tools
- Turnover replacement: 2-3 months of pay per turnover event

**All-in monthly cost:** $800-3,600 per month

### Eastern European VA (Romania, Poland, Ukraine)

**Sticker price:** $15-25 per hour
**20 hours/week:** $1,200-2,000 per month

**Hidden costs:** Same structure as offshore, slightly higher tool costs due to timezone-appropriate software

**All-in monthly cost:** $2,400-6,000 per month

### US-Based VA

**Sticker price:** $25-50 per hour
**20 hours/week:** $2,000-4,000 per month

**Hidden costs:** Higher management costs (US VAs expect more direction), higher tool costs, potentially higher turnover

**All-in monthly cost:** $4,000-12,000 per month

The [real cost of a virtual assistant](/blog/va-vs-platform/virtual-assistant-real-cost) goes deeper on each line item. But let's break down where these numbers come from.

## Training: The cost nobody budgets for

Every new VA needs training. You cannot hand them your business and expect them to know how it works.

**Initial training:** 20-40 hours over the first 2-4 weeks.

That's not their time. That's YOUR time. Time spent recording Loom videos. Writing SOPs. Walking through processes on Zoom. Reviewing their first attempts at tasks. Correcting mistakes. Answering questions.

If you bill at $200 per hour and spend 30 hours training a new VA, that's $6,000 in opportunity cost. Not out-of-pocket. But real. You're not doing billable work during those hours. You're training.

**Ongoing training:** 2-4 hours per month for process updates, new tools, course corrections.

That's another $400-800 per month at $200/hour.

Most VA job postings don't mention this. The VA platforms don't highlight it. But every business owner who's hired a VA discovers it in month one.

## Management overhead: The part-time job you didn't ask for

Hiring a VA doesn't eliminate your operational work. It transforms it. Instead of doing the tasks yourself, you're now managing someone who does them.

**Weekly management time:** 3-5 hours per week.

Breaking this down:
- 30 minutes reviewing completed work
- 30 minutes answering questions via Slack or email
- 1 hour in weekly sync calls
- 1-3 hours providing direction, correcting course, re-doing work that wasn't quite right

At $200 per hour, that's $600-1,000 per week. $2,400-4,000 per month.

This is the cost that surprises people most. They thought they were buying freedom. Instead, they bought a management responsibility.

## Tool seats: The subscription creep

Your VA needs access to your systems. Every tool you use that has per-user pricing just got more expensive.

**Typical tool seat costs:**

| Tool | Monthly Seat Cost |
|------|------------------|
| CRM (HubSpot, Dubsado) | $20-50 |
| Scheduling (Calendly Teams) | $16/user |
| Email marketing (Mailchimp) | $20-50 |
| Project management (Asana, ClickUp) | $11-30 |
| Password manager (1Password, LastPass) | $8-20 |
| Communication (Slack) | $8-15 |

**Total:** $80-600 per month depending on your stack.

Some business owners share logins to avoid this. Don't. It's a security nightmare and violates most terms of service.

## Turnover: The reset cost

Here's the number that really matters. The average VA tenure is 6-12 months. They leave for better opportunities, different timezones, or personal reasons.

When they leave, you lose:

1. **Institutional knowledge.** The processes that lived in their head are gone.
2. **Productivity.** You're back to doing everything yourself while you find a replacement.
3. **Training investment.** All those hours you spent training? Gone. You start over.

**Turnover cost:** 2-3 months of VA pay, plus 20-40 hours of re-training time.

For a $2,000/month VA, that's $4,000-6,000 per turnover event. For a $4,000/month US VA, that's $8,000-12,000.

Since turnover happens every 6-12 months, budget for it. It's not an "if." It's a "when."

## The 12-month true cost comparison

Let's put this all together for a realistic scenario.

**Scenario:** You hire a Filipino VA at $10/hour for 20 hours/week. Sticker price: $800/month.

| Cost Category | Monthly | Annual |
|---------------|---------|--------|
| Base pay | $800 | $9,600 |
| Training (amortized) | $400 | $4,800 |
| Management time | $1,200 | $14,400 |
| Tool seats | $200 | $2,400 |
| Turnover reserve (1x/year) | $400 | $4,800 |
| **Total** | **$3,000** | **$36,000** |

That $10/hour VA actually costs $37.50/hour all-in.

Now compare that to an operations platform at $97-497/month with zero training, zero management, zero tool seats, and zero turnover. The math changes dramatically.

## What this means for your decision

The point of this breakdown isn't to say "never hire a VA." The point is to budget accurately and compare apples to apples.

**If you hire a VA:**
- Budget 2-3x the sticker price
- Set aside time for training and management
- Plan for turnover every 6-12 months
- Document everything to reduce knowledge loss

**If you use a platform:**
- Fixed cost: $97-497/month
- Zero training time
- Near-zero management time
- No turnover risk
- No tool seat creep

For the $3,000/month all-in VA cost, you could run a full operations platform at $497/month and still have $2,500/month left over for a part-time VA to handle the high-value human work.

That's the hybrid model. And for most service businesses, it's the right answer.

## FAQ

### What is the real cost of a $10/hour virtual assistant?

A $10/hour VA at 20 hrs/week costs $800/month base pay. All-in cost: $3,000/month after training ($400), management ($1,200), tool seats ($200), and turnover reserve ($400). True hourly cost: $37.50/hour.

### How much does a US virtual assistant cost per month?

US VA sticker price: $25-50/hour ($2,000-4,000/month at 20 hrs/week). All-in cost: $4,000-12,000/month. Training takes 20-40 hours. Management takes 3-5 hours/week. Turnover happens every 6-12 months.

### What are the hidden costs of hiring a VA?

Training: 20-40 hours of your time. Management: 3-5 hours/week ongoing. Tool seats: $80-600/month across 4-6 tools. Turnover: 2-3 months of pay per replacement. All-in cost is 2-3x the sticker price.

### How long does the average VA stay in their position?

6-12 months average tenure. Turnover costs include recruiting (5-10 hours), re-training (20-40 hours), and productivity loss (2-4 weeks). Budget for 1-2 turnover events per year.

### Is a VA or automation platform cheaper?

VA: $3,000-12,000/month all-in. Platform: $97-497/month fixed. For repetitive tasks (follow-ups, invoicing, scheduling), platforms are 10x cheaper. For judgment work, VAs add value. Hybrid model (platform + part-time VA) is most efficient.
