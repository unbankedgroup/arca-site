Testimonials are social proof. They close deals. They build trust. They differentiate you from coaches who can't prove their results.

Yet most coaches have maybe 3-5 testimonials. Scattered across emails. Outdated. Hard to find.

Testimonial collection automation changes this. Strategic timing. Easy process. Consistent requests. All automated.

This guide covers the testimonial sequence that generates 40-60% response rates. With templates. With timing. With the psychology that makes it work.

## When to ask for testimonials

Timing matters. Ask at the wrong moment and you'll get a polite decline.

**Best testimonial request windows:**

| Trigger | Timing | Response Rate |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| Mid-package win | Session 4-6 | 30-40% |
| Package completion | Final session | 40-60% |
| Specific breakthrough | Immediately after | 50-70% |
| Follow-up success | 30-60 days post | 20-30% |

The sweet spot is package completion. They've finished the work. Results are visible. Satisfaction is high.

## The testimonial request sequence

Here's the exact sequence that works.

### Message 1: The setup (Final session)

**Purpose:** Mention testimonials in person. Warm up the ask.

**Script:**
```
"As we wrap up today, I wanted to ask something.

I'm always looking to improve my work. And I'd love to hear about your experience — both what worked and what didn't.

Would you be open to sharing a few thoughts? I'll send over a short form. Takes about 2 minutes.

No pressure at all. But if you're willing, it would mean a lot."
```

**Why it works:** You're asking in person. It's harder to say no. You're framing it as feedback, not a testimonial.

### Message 2: The request (Day 1-2 post-completion)

**Purpose:** Make the ask. Easy process.

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

Great session yesterday. Congratulations on completing the package!

As I mentioned, I'd love to hear about your experience. Here's a quick form:

[Testimonial form link]

Takes about 2 minutes. Questions like:
- What was your biggest win?
- What surprised you about the work?
- Would you recommend me? To who?

No pressure. But if you're willing, it would mean a lot.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Specific time commitment. Clear questions. Easy opt-out.

### Message 3: The reminder (Day 5-7 if no response)

**Purpose:** Gentle nudge. No guilt.

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

Just floating this to the top of your inbox in case you missed it.

If you have 2 minutes to share your experience, I'd really appreciate it.

[Form link]

If you're swamped, no worries at all. Just didn't want it to slip through.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Friendly. Understanding. Easy to act on.

### Message 4: The thank-you (When they submit)

**Purpose:** Acknowledge. Build goodwill.

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

Thank you so much for sharing your experience! It means a lot.

I'm so glad the work resonated. And I appreciate you taking the time to tell others about it.

If I can ever return the favor, let me know.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Genuine gratitude. Relationship building.

## Making it easy

The biggest barrier to testimonials is effort. Reduce the friction.

**Use a simple form:**
- 3-5 questions max
- Mix of multiple choice and open text
- Mobile-friendly

**Sample questions:**
1. What was your biggest win from our work together?
2. What surprised you about the coaching process?
3. What would you tell someone considering working with me?
4. On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to recommend me?
5. Can I quote you? (Yes/No)

**Offer alternatives:**
- Video testimonial (record a 2-min Loom)
- Email testimonial (just reply to this email)
- Call testimonial (15-min chat, you transcribe)

Give them options. Some people hate forms.

## The incentive (optional)

Some coaches offer incentives. Some don't.

**Good incentives:**
- Entry into a quarterly gift card drawing
- Discount on next package (5-10%)
- Bonus resource or worksheet

**Avoid:**
- Large incentives (attracts fake testimonials)
- Contingent rewards (only if positive)
- Cash payments (feels transactional)

The best incentive is genuine gratitude. But a small thank-you doesn't hurt.

## Using the testimonials

Collecting is step one. Using them is step two.

**Where to put testimonials:**
- Website homepage
- Services/sales page
- Email signature
- Social media posts
- Proposal documents
- Discovery call follow-ups

**How to format:**
- Full name + title (with permission)
- First name only (if preferred)
- Anonymous (if requested)

Always ask permission before using. Most clients will say yes.
