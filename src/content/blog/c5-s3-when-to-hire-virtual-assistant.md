The question isn't "VA or platform?" The question is "VA or platform at my current stage?"

When to hire a virtual assistant vs. use an operations platform depends on three things: your revenue, your task mix, and your available bandwidth for management.

A founder at $50K/year has different constraints than a founder at $500K/year. The right decision at one stage is the wrong decision at another.

This guide breaks down the decision by revenue stage. Not because revenue is the only factor. But because it's a proxy for everything that matters: budget, volume, complexity, and opportunity cost.

## Under $100K annual revenue

**Your reality:** You're building. Every dollar needs to work hard. You probably can't afford a good VA without sacrificing other priorities.

**Typical challenges:**
- Leads going cold because you're delivering work
- Invoices sent late (or forgotten)
- No consistent follow-up system
- You're doing 100% of operations yourself

**The VA math at this stage:**

A Filipino VA at $800-1,200/month sounds affordable. But the all-in cost is $2,400-3,600/month when you count training, management, tool seats, and turnover reserve.

At $100K/year revenue ($8,300/month), that's 29-43% of your revenue going to one VA. That's not sustainable.

**The platform math at this stage:**

An operations platform at $97-247/month is 1-3% of your revenue. It handles the repeatable work: lead response, invoicing, follow-ups, scheduling.

You don't need a VA yet. You need infrastructure.

**My recommendation:**

Start with a platform. Build your operational foundation first. Arca's Foundation plan at $97/month handles:
- Lead response within 5 minutes
- Invoice generation and payment reminders
- Client onboarding sequences
- Daily briefings
- Appointment confirmations

At this stage, every dollar needs to multiply. A platform gives you 168 hours/week of operational coverage for $97/month. A VA gives you 40 hours/week for 10-30x the cost.

**When to add a VA at this stage:**

Wait until you hit $150-200K/year OR until you have consistent overflow work that a platform can't handle. Think:
- Content creation (blogs, social posts, newsletters)
- Research projects
- Complex client communications that need a human touch

Until then, the platform runs your operations. You run your business.

## $100K-$300K annual revenue

**Your reality:** You can afford help. But you need to be strategic. Every dollar still needs to work hard.

**Typical challenges:**
- You're at 8-15 clients and things are slipping
- You're spending 15-20 hours/week on admin
- You know you need help but don't know what kind
- You're losing revenue to dropped follow-ups and late invoices

**The VA math at this stage:**

At $200K/year ($16,600/month), a $3,000/month VA is 18% of revenue. Manageable. But is it optimal?

If the VA spends 60-70% of their time on automatable tasks, you're spending $1,800-2,100/month on work a platform does for $97-247/month.

**The platform math at this stage:**

A platform at $247/month (Structure tier) handles the operational backbone. You have $2,750/month left over from the VA budget.

What can you do with $2,750/month?
- Hire a part-time VA for 10-15 hours/week at $600-900/month
- Still have $1,850-2,150/month for other priorities

**My recommendation:**

Hybrid model. Platform for operations. Part-time VA for high-value work.

**Platform handles:**
- Lead response and qualification
- Invoice generation and payment chasing
- Client onboarding flows
- Follow-up sequences
- Daily briefings

**VA handles (10-15 hours/week):**
- Personalized lead outreach
- Client relationship check-ins
- Content creation
- Research and preparation work
- Complex scheduling (events, groups, special requests)

This model gives you comprehensive coverage for under $2,000/month. And your VA is doing work that actually moves the needle — not sending invoice reminders.

**When to go full-time VA at this stage:**

Consider a full-time VA if:
- You have 20+ hours/week of genuinely high-value work (not automatable ops)
- You're managing a team and need operational coordination
- You have complex client relationships that need daily human attention

Otherwise, hybrid wins.

## $300K+ annual revenue

**Your reality:** You have budget for both. The question is efficiency and risk management.

**Typical challenges:**
- Multiple VAs creating management overhead
- Turnover risk as operations scale
- Inconsistency across different people handling processes
- You're spending more time managing than leading

**The VA math at this stage:**

At $500K/year ($41,600/month), a $4,000/month US-based VA is 10% of revenue. Totally manageable.

But if you need 2-3 VAs to cover all your operations, you're looking at $10,000-12,000/month. Plus 10-15 hours/week of your time managing them.

**The platform math at this stage:**

A platform at $497/month (Fortress tier) handles the entire operational backbone. You have $9,500-11,500/month left for VAs who focus on high-value work.

**My recommendation:**

Full platform for operations. VA or part-time ops person for high-value human work. Consider a managed operations approach where the platform runs the systems and a human oversees strategy.

**Platform handles:**
- Everything in the Structure tier, plus:
- Advanced reporting and analytics
- Custom integrations
- Priority support
- Multi-user access for your team

**VA/ops person handles:**
- Strategic client communications
- Team coordination
- Process improvement
- Exception handling
- Relationship management at the highest level

This model scales efficiently. Adding more clients doesn't require more VAs. The platform handles the volume increase. You add human capacity only where judgment is required.

**When this model breaks:**

At $1M+ revenue, you might need a full operations team. But even then, the platform should be the backbone. Humans oversee, strategize, and handle exceptions. The platform executes.

## The task-based decision (regardless of revenue)

Revenue is a proxy. The real decision is task-based.

**Automate these tasks at any revenue level:**
- Lead response within 5 minutes
- Invoice generation and payment reminders
- Appointment confirmations and reminders
- Client onboarding sequences
- Follow-up sequences (post-session, post-package, re-engagement)
- Daily briefings

**Hire a VA for these tasks at any revenue level:**
- Complex client communications
- Creative content development
- Strategic research and analysis
- Relationship building with high-value clients
- Exception handling and problem-solving

**Use a hybrid approach for:**
- Qualified lead outreach (platform qualifies, VA personalizes)
- Client reporting (platform compiles, VA comments)
- Meeting prep (platform pulls data, VA adds strategy)
- Proposal creation (platform templates, VA customizes)

## The transition timeline

Regardless of your revenue stage, here's how to transition.

**Week 1-2: Audit.** List every task. Categorize as automatable, human-required, or hybrid.

**Week 3-4: Automate the automatable.** Set up the platform for Category 1 tasks. Run parallel with your current setup.

**Week 5-6: Transition.** Move Category 1 tasks fully to the platform. Re-deploy VA time to Category 2 and 3 work.

**Week 7+: Optimize.** Evaluate what's working. Adjust. Add more automation where it makes sense.

The [7-Day Build](/blog/managed-operations/7-day-build-explained) compresses this for most businesses. But the gradual approach works too.

## The bottom line

Under $100K: Platform first. VA later.
$100K-300K: Hybrid model. Platform for ops, part-time VA for high-value work.
$300K+: Full platform, full VA or ops person for strategic work.

Revenue isn't the only factor. But it's the best proxy for what you can afford and what you need.

Match your model to your stage. Then scale from there.

## FAQ

### At what revenue should I hire my first VA?

Wait until $150-200K/year revenue. Under $100K, use a platform first ($97-247/month). At $100-300K, use hybrid model (platform + part-time VA). At $300K+, full platform + full-time VA for strategic work.

### How much revenue do I need to afford a VA?

Real cost of VA is 2-3x sticker price. Filipino VA: $2,400-3,600/month all-in. US VA: $4,000-12,000/month all-in. At $8,300/month revenue ($100K/year), a VA consumes 29-43% of revenue. Not sustainable until $150K+.

### What should I automate before hiring a VA?

Lead response within 5 minutes, invoice generation, payment reminders, appointment confirmations, client onboarding sequences, follow-up sequences. These are rule-based and run better on a platform.

### When is a VA better than automation?

When work requires judgment, creativity, or relationship depth. Complex client communications, strategic research, creative content, exception handling, and tasks that change daily need a human.

### What's the hybrid VA model?

Platform for operations (60-70% of tasks). VA for judgment work (30-40%). Costs 50-60% less than VA-only. Scales better. VA does higher-value work instead of invoice reminders.
